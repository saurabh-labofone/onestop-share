Caption — Instagram post body

Everyone wants the guy who called the bottom. Howard Marks will tell you, plainly, that no such guy exists. He didn't predict 2008. He's said so himself: markets kept falling after his firm started buying.

So what actually happened? Oaktree raised an ~$11B war chest in 2007 — before the crash. Then, in the last 15 weeks of 2008, while the world was selling, the firm deployed more than $600 million a week. Not because they knew where the bottom was. Because they knew where they were in the cycle: maximum fear, maximum bargains.

Here's the part people skip. The edge wasn't a prediction. It was preparation plus a stomach for pain — buying while positions kept dropping, with capital locked up for years. "We may never know where we're going, but we'd better have a good idea where we are."

The system sells you forecasts because forecasts feel like control. Marks built a career on the opposite: you can't predict, you can prepare. That's not a stock tip. It's a temperament. And temperament is the one thing no finfluencer can sell you.
