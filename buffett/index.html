Caption — Instagram post body

In 2007, Warren Buffett bet $1,000,000 that a plain S&P 500 index fund would beat the best hedge funds money could buy. Protégé Partners took the other side and picked five funds-of-hedge-funds — over 100 elite managers in total.

Ten years later (2008–2017), it wasn't close. The index fund returned +125.8%. The hedge funds averaged +36.3%. The simplest option on the table beat the most sophisticated by nearly 3.5×.

Why? A big part of it was fees. The hedge funds charged "2-and-20" — 2% of your money every year plus 20% of any gains, win or lose, often layered twice. The index fund charged 0.04%. Sophistication isn't free; simplicity nearly is.

Here's the part most people miss: Buffett didn't pick stocks for this bet. He picked the index. The lesson isn't "Buffett is smart" — it's "the index is hard to beat." And the exact fund he used is available to anyone with a brokerage account. No skill. No special access. The same fund.

The winnings — which grew to about $2.2M while invested — went to Girls Inc. of Omaha.

Sources: Berkshire Hathaway 2017 shareholder letter; Long Bets Foundation. Education only, not investment advice. Past performance does not guarantee future results.
