Caption — Instagram post body

He picks stocks the way most people pick friends. He finds a few he believes in, holds them for a decade-plus, and barely checks on them. In 2004-05 he loaded up on three obscure small-caps — Atul Auto, Aegis Logistics, Cera Sanitaryware — and then mostly just sat. Over the next 10-12 years all three rose more than 100x. (Wikipedia)

Here's the part nobody puts on the highlight reel. By his own account, that patience came after roughly 12 years of losing money trading futures. He pawned his mother's jewellery. There were months he couldn't afford milk for his son. The calm came AFTER the wipe-out — it didn't replace it.

His mantra, posted on his own accounts: "Invest like a bull, sit like a bear, watch like an eagle." Easy to screenshot. Brutal to live.

And "barely checks on them" cuts both ways. That same conviction means a concentrated small-cap book that swings hard. His disclosed holdings (listed stakes above 1%, not total net worth) ran near ₹1,896 cr at the peak and sat around ₹967 cr by the March 2026 quarter — roughly half, gone, on paper. Most of us would have panic-sold somewhere in that drop. He didn't.

We don't roast Kedia for that. We roast the legend that sells you the holding without the wipe-out, and the survivorship trap that only ever names the winners. Patience isn't a personality. It's a thing he paid for. Close the app. Go for a walk.
