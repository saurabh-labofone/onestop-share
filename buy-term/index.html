Caption — Instagram post body

You were sold, not advised. The man you trusted — often a relative — made around ₹14,000 the day you signed, because that's what the product was built to do.

It has many names: endowment, money-back, "guaranteed savings plan." They're all the same thing — insurance and investment glued into one product, so the commission can hide in the join. On a ₹50,000/year endowment the first-year agent commission is roughly 28% — about ₹14,000.

Split it the smart way instead — a term plan for cover, a low-cost index fund for growth — and the agent's cut drops to around ₹3,000. Same money in; the rest stays invested for you.

So why did he push the worse one? Because nobody earns a commission telling you to do the right thing — not even the uncle who swears by it. He isn't lying to you; he was sold the same story, and paid to pass it on. Don't blame the agent. Blame the incentive. Then go buy term.
