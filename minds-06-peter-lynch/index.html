Caption — Instagram post body

Everyone remembers the Peter Lynch headline: invest in what you know. The supermarket pick. The pantyhose that beat the analysts.

Here's the part that actually matters. Lynch ran Fidelity's Magellan fund to 29.2% a year for 13 years — roughly double the market. And Fidelity's own numbers suggest the typical investor in that fund earned closer to 7%. Same fund. Same manager. The gap was entirely behavior: piling in after good years, bailing in the dips.

So no, the lesson isn't "go find the next L'eggs." You can own the best fund on the planet and still lose to it, if you can't sit through one ugly year.

And the famous L'eggs story? He didn't just take the tip from his wife. He went and did the homework on why it sold. The noticing was step one. The holding was the whole game.

Markets will have a bad week while you read this. Your plan is probably fine. Save this for the next time you're tempted to touch it.
