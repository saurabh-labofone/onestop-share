Caption — Instagram post body

Free money. And you're probably saying no to some of it — every payday, without even knowing it.

Most employers offer a 401(k) match: for every $1 you put in, they add 50¢ — usually up to 6% of your salary. On a $60,000 salary, that's $1,800 a year in free money, handed to you on top of what you save. Put another way, it's an instant 50% return — guaranteed, before the market does anything. Nothing else in finance comes close.

And yet about 1 in 4 workers don't contribute enough to capture the full match — roughly $24 billion in employer money left unclaimed every year. The reason usually isn't greed or laziness. It's a default: the enrollment form set your contribution below the match threshold, and nobody told you to change it.

Left alone for a career, that's loud math — $1,800 a year, compounding at 7%, is about $360,000 over 40 years. The cost of a form nobody fixed.

So this week: open your 401(k) and check your contribution rate. Make sure you're putting in at least enough to get the full match. It's the one raise you can give yourself today.
