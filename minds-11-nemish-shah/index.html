Caption — Instagram post body

Some investors build a brand. Nemish Shah built a wall — between himself and the noise.

Forty years at ENAM. Near-zero interviews. No TV, no socials, no press conference even when the Axis deal closed. While his partner met the promoters and fronted the IPOs, Shah sat in a room and read annual reports. A handful of stocks. Held for a decade-plus each. Roughly ₹3,000 crore in disclosed holdings — and that's only the part he was legally required to show.

Here's the part the legend skips: the silence wasn't the secret. Someone else absorbed the noise so he could afford to stay quiet. Copy "say nothing, hold forever" on your own and you've got no such shield — plus we only ever see the multibaggers, never the dead years he sat through. The edge was never the silence. It was the discipline behind it: a hard floor on return on capital, and reading the management behind every number.

Markets will be loud this week. Your plan probably doesn't need a single post about it. Close the app. Go read something boring and true.
