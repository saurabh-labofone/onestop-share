Caption — Instagram post body

In 2008, the crash wiped out nearly a third of Rakesh Jhunjhunwala's fortune — hundreds of crores, gone in months. He didn't sell a thing. By 2012 he'd earned all of it back.

That's the whole story of India's "Big Bull." He started in 1985 with ₹5,000 and died in 2022 worth about $5.8 billion — and almost none of it came from trading fast. One stock made the legend: he bought Titan around ₹30 in 2003 and simply never sold. Twenty years later it was up roughly 82 times.

His rule, in his own words: "Bad times are good times for those who keep their nerve."

Everyone copies the buy. The buy was the easy part. The hard part was the dead years — Titan going sideways, then crashing in 2008, while everyone told him he was wrong. He just didn't flinch.

So: when did you last sell something good just because it stopped moving — and what did you do with the money instead?
