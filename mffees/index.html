Caption — Instagram post body

Your mutual fund grows 12% a year. You keep 10.5%.

That missing 1.5% has a name: the expense ratio. It's the fee the fund takes to run itself, pulled out of your money automatically, every year. No SMS. No bill. You never feel it leave.

Here's what it actually costs. Put ₹10 lakh in for 20 years at the same 12% market return: a cheap index fund (0.20% fee) leaves you ₹93.08 lakh; a typical active fund (1.50% fee) leaves ₹73.66 lakh. Same money, same market, ₹19.41 lakh difference. That's purely the fee.

And you often pay more to get less. 79 of every 100 active large-cap funds in India lost to their benchmark over the last 10 years (SPIVA India, Year-End 2025). ULIPs are worse: on that same ₹10 lakh, you'd finish roughly ₹31.66 lakh behind the index.

The check takes two minutes. Open any fund's factsheet and find one line: the Total Expense Ratio (TER). Compare it before you put money in. A lower fee means more of the market's return stays yours.
